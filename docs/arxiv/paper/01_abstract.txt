On NVIDIA H100 PCIe (driver 550.163), a user-space CUDA shim packing 512 KiB logical KV bands into 2 MiB driver leaves reduces committed VRAM by 42.2% at a 70% VRAM budget fill while delivering 131% higher budget-curve logical KV than stock at the same tier (50%/60%: 19.1%/32.6% liberation). The headline gain is a budget-curve ratio; matched-slot F1' resident stress shows 0.15% slot gain and 100% vs 70.3% layout efficiency (mechanism proof, not 131%). LD_PRELOAD on cuMem* without source changes. Synthetic KV-band microbenchmark with GATE12 and Docker repro isolates allocator/driver-layer behavior from framework paging (vLLM).
